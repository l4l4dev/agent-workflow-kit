# Human Decision

## Decision

The Human owner adopts Option B as the closed deliverable boundary for the future TASK-2.3 implementation step that will establish the TASK-1.8 governing records.

That closed deliverable set contains only:

- `records/TASK-1.8/definitions/DEF-001.md`
- `records/TASK-1.8/decisions/HD-001.md`
- `records/TASK-1.8/index.md`

No separate retrospective Human Decision or other Canonical Record may be created to duplicate or retrospectively republish the bootstrap-period decisions.

This `TASK-2.3/HD-002` is the current Option B authorization record. It must not reproduce, replace, supersede, convert, migrate, or retrospectively reclassify the committed bootstrap artifacts. Those artifacts remain the authoritative historical sources for the bootstrap-period decisions they preserve:

- `BOOTSTRAP_TASK_2_1.md`
- `BOOTSTRAP_TASK_2_2.md`
- `BOOTSTRAP_TASK_2_2_ARCHITECTURE_DECISION.md`

The TASK-1.8 retrospective records may cite those committed bootstrap artifacts as historical evidence where relevant. They must distinguish:

- verified historical facts supported by committed repository evidence;
- the current Human Decision governing whether and how TASK-1.8 may continue; and
- information that is unavailable or cannot be verified.

Historical approval, scope, Acceptance Criteria, Evidence, Review results, dates, chronology, or authority must not be inferred or reconstructed without repository evidence.

## Rationale

Repository First is already satisfied for the bootstrap-period decisions because the bootstrap artifacts are committed and repository-resident. A duplicate retrospective record would add neither missing Evidence nor missing authority.

Duplication would weaken historical traceability by separating the decisions from their original repository context and would create interpretive ambiguity about whether a later record was a current decision, a retrospective republication, or a replacement for temporary bootstrap governance. Option B avoids that ambiguity and is the smallest sufficient deliverable set for the safe continuation of TASK-1.8.

## Scope and Limitations

This decision establishes only the closed future deliverable boundary stated in the Decision section. It does not yet authorize creation of any of the three TASK-1.8 files.

This `TASK-2.3/HD-002` is the repository-resident Human Decision required by the already approved scope of `TASK-2.3/DEF-001`. It establishes the closed implementation boundary required before the future TASK-1.8 governing records may be created, and it does not expand the approved scope of TASK-2.3. For avoidance of doubt, Acceptance Criterion 26 of `TASK-2.3/DEF-001` constrains the initial TASK-2.3 governing-record creation authorized by Stage 1 of `TASK-2.3/HD-001`; it does not prohibit this later Human Decision required to carry out the already approved TASK-2.3 scope.

This decision does not authorize:

- any additional retrospective or Canonical Record;
- modification of any existing Canonical Record, Canonical specification, template, or bootstrap artifact;
- modification, staging, commit, Finalization, or other disposition of the existing untracked `CANONICAL_LIFECYCLE.md`;
- Independent Review, Remediation, Independent Re-Review, or Finalization for TASK-1.8;
- validation implementation, CLI behavior, workflow automation, or external-system mutation;
- staging, commit, or push.

Creation of the three TASK-1.8 files requires a separate explicit Human Implementation Authorization after this decision enters the authoritative committed repository state. The future `TASK-1.8/DEF-001` must not approve itself. The future `TASK-1.8/HD-001` must explicitly identify and approve `TASK-1.8/DEF-001` and must determine whether and how the existing untracked `CANONICAL_LIFECYCLE.md` may continue.
